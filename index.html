Mary Slessor

The woman who stopped the killing of twins in Calabar, Nigeria.

Mary Slessor was a dedicated missionary in the late 1800s to early 1900s, with a vigor and passion that brought the Lord’s Kingdom to Africa. She is now noted as one of Africa’s top missionaries, and much of West Africa was evangelized due to the open door that Mary created for the next generation of missionaries to follow after her. Born in 1848 to a Scottish family, Mary would go on to transform the world with the Gospel.

Here is a Timeline of Mary Slessor's Life

  • 1848 - Born in Aberdeen, Scotland
  • 1859 - Family moved to Dundee
  • 1875 - Joined the Missionary
  • 1876- Came to Calabar in Nigeria, Africa
  • 1879 - Went back to Dundee because she contracted Malaria in Calabar
  • 1880- Returned to Calabar
  • 1883 - Returned to Scotland because of another health issue
  • 1885- Returned on her third mission to Calabar, Her Mother and sister died
  • 1888 - Moved to Okoyong in Calabar, Adopted a lot of abandoned twins because twins were considered evil and left alone to die.
  • 1898 - Went back to Scotland with four of her adopted children
  • 1903 - Came to Arochukwu
  • 1905 - She was named the Vice President of Ikot Obong native court
  • 1908 - Began a home for women and girls at Use
  • 1913- She received the Royal Medal
  • 1915 - Died at the age of 65 after illness
